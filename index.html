What I thought of Share a Story

I registered for this class in hopes of becoming more knowledgeable about news. Using Share a Story has made me more mindful of how I consume news–specifically the who, what, when, where, and why–so I can share and journal more intentionally. While it was difficult to share everyday because of other classes and activities, as a Game Design student, I’d find it more enticing if there were game-like elements. Despite this, I felt that the task of sharing was easy enough to complete almost everyday because it only took 5-10mins. As feedback to future students, I would recommend downloading many different news apps, so they can easily access different sources.

Sentiment Breakdown

In this section, I classified my news as “good” or “bad” because I wanted to know which I read more of. I wondered if I avoided certain news. However, this classification was difficult because what does good and bad mean? Every article has Pros and Cons that are good for some people but not for others. So I based it off how I felt: it’s “good news” if a crime is resolved, the consumer wins, and if the news isn’t bad, then it has to be good. I was surprised to see the results because I thought I shared more “good news”, since I remember actively trying to avoid sharing “bad news” sometimes to be more positive.

Emotional Reactions

How stories made you feel

After reviewing my articles’ “This matters because…” data, I tried to remember how I felt when reading each story–analyzing how I worded each response. Based on this data it appears that I mostly felt curious, often from learning something new.

Topic Distribution

I also associated each story with a topic. My most frequent topic was culture and technology, and I was surprised that politics as 3rd since I often tried to avoid sharing political stories. I would do this by scrolling to the bottom and picking something that interested me–often not reading more than 1-2 articles before sharing. However, this could be due to the time constraints, since political stories usually show first on my feed, if I had very little time left to share, I would feel inclined to share the first thing I saw.

News Diet Diversity Score

Most of my stories are from the NYT, so I think I have a higher risk of echo chamber due to lack of diversity and seeking of other perspectives. While this may be attributed to being a student in NY and growing up in MA, and it could also be because of the time constraint. Oftentimes, I found myself continuously using the same news sources to share on time. Although NYT is considered “high-quality” journalism, it only shows one editorial lens.

Discovery Type Breakdown

Typically, I shared stories from directly browsing NYT vs. happening to hear of the news from my environment. However, whenever I heard news in the world, I would share it, but it didn’t happen very often. I wonder if this is because of who I surround myself with or how often I go out. Or maybe, I did hear news, but I hadn’t considered it news-worthy.
